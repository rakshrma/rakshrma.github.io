I am a biomedical engineer who has been involved in multidisciplinary projects. During undergraduate degree, I fabricated a pressure sensor embedded contact lens. I worked on the development of osteoconductive bone cement for faster bone healing for my master's dissertation. During my industrial experience, I developed AI-enabled android application for disease diagnosis and optimized immunoassay platforms for harmone analyis. I also co-founded a surgical robotics startup to assist sugeons perform accurate intial puncture for laproscopy. In my current role of data scientist at Eli Lilly, I got an opportunity to work with Prof. Bouman and Prof. Buzzard from Purdue on model-based iterative reconstruction. It re-kindled my interest in image formation and image analysis. With an aim to build career in research, I am currently seeking graduate student position.

I am currently a postdoc/contractor at the Northeast Fisheries Science Center, where I work with Dr. Tim Miller to extend state-space stock assessment models to incorporate environmental effects on life-history attributes (WHAM). I completed my Ph.D. with Dr. Brice Semmens at Scripps Institution of Oceanography, UC San Diego.
